Romania

"Romania" derives from the local name for Romanian (Romanian: român), which in turn derives from Latin romanus, meaning "Roman" or "of Rome". This ethnonym for Romanians is first attested in the 16th century by Italian humanists travelling in Transylvania, Moldavia, and Wallachia.

Corvins Castle

It is one of the largest castles in Europe and is featured as one of the Seven Wonders of Romania

The Ice Hotel

Ice hotels are dependent upon sub-freezing temperatures (colder than 0 °C) during construction and operation.

Decebalus

is a colossal carving of the face of Decebalus (AD 87-106), the last king of Dacia, who fought against the Roman emperors

Danube Delta

The Danube Delta is the second largest river delta in Europe, after the Volga Delta, and is the best preserved on the continent.
